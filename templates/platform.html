How the AHP Model Works — Step by Step
Step 1
Live Evidence
Real market data fetched: β, σ, Max Drawdown, Expected Return, Dividend Yield for all 7 asset classes.
Step 2
Pairwise Matrices
AI compares each asset pair on every sub-criterion. "Is Large Stocks safer than Small Stocks on Volatility?" — fills 7×7 matrices.
Step 3
Criteria Scores
Sub-criteria scores are synthesised into Return, Risk, Liquidity, and Diversification scores per asset using the geometric mean method.
Step 4
Criteria Weighting
The 4 criteria are compared pairwise. Return ≈ 40%, Risk ≈ 35%, Liquidity ≈ 15%, Diversification ≈ 10%.
Step 5
Final Allocation
AHP synthesises all levels into portfolio weights, applies policy constraints, and validates with 1,000 Monte Carlo simulations.
